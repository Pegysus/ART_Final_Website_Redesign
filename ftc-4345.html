FTC 4345

Our team, 4345 is the official school team of Aragon High School in San Mateo, CA. We have been a student-run and student-led team since Aragon Robotics started doing FTC in 2010. This year, the FTC team consists of 13 people.

This year our team has developed in many ways. We significantly expanded our outreach and media efforts to recruit new members and make connections with our local community. We have revamped our website, restarted our YouTube channel, and utilized multiple social media platforms to record our experiences as a team. Outreach events we’ve done this year include demonstrations for local middle schoolers (future team members), creating and running introductory robotics programs for local Girl and Boy Scout troops, presenting at San Mateo County Faire, and participating in other events hosted by our school and our sponsors. We hope that we can inspire younger generations by teaching them about the value of building robots and the excitement and joy we experience in the process.

We also streamlined our design process throughout this season. We decided to focus on custom-built parts this year more than previous years because they are customizable and more germane than TETRIX parts. Since we have access to multiple 3D printers, the whole team worked to become familiar with CAD throughout the season, collaborating through Fusion 360’s inbuilt cloud-sharing system. The whole design process going from basic prototypes to complex, efficient subsystems to a functioning robot and can be seen in our CAD documentation.
